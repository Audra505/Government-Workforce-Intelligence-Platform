# Background

## Current Intelligence State

The platform has three tenant-wide intelligence signals (Vacancy Risk,
Workforce Readiness, Attrition Risk) and a working `/intelligence`
workspace with Workforce Signals and Vacancy Risk tabs. The Department Gap
tab exists in the UI as a disabled, non-interactive placeholder. No
department-level computation of any kind exists anywhere in the backend.

## What FR-411 and the Attrition Reporting Rules Actually Require

FR-411 names three levels: Agency Readiness (done, M31), Department
Readiness (not done), Workforce Readiness (the metric name itself, done).
`directives/06_attrition_scoring_rules.md` separately requires attrition
reporting to support Department Risk alongside Agency Risk. Both
requirements point at the same underlying need: take the existing,
already-governed Readiness and Attrition formulas and run them per
department instead of per tenant. This decision authorizes exactly that,
and nothing more.

## Why This Is Not a New Scoring Model

`WorkforceReadinessService` and `AttritionRiskService` already scope every
query to `tenantId`. Department-level scoring changes the population filter
from "everyone in the tenant" to "everyone in the tenant and this
department" — the weights, thresholds, confidence rules, and formula
version identifiers defined in `GD-M31-1` Decision 5 and `GD-M32-1`
Decision 5 are unchanged. No new weight, no new factor, no new threshold is
introduced by this decision.

# Decisions

## Decision 1 — Scope Boundary: Department-Level Extension of Two Existing Signals, Plus Vacancy Context

M33 authorizes exactly three things, combined into a single milestone
because they answer the same product question ("how is this department
doing?") and share the same suppression safeguard:

```text
1. Department-level Workforce Readiness — reusing GD-M31-1 Decision 5's
   formula verbatim, re-scoped per department
2. Department-level Attrition Risk — reusing GD-M32-1 Decision 5's formula
   verbatim, re-scoped per department
3. Department-level vacancy/capacity context — reusing VacancyRiskService's
   existing per-vacancy department data, aggregated (counts and averages
   only, no new score)
```

No fourth signal, no new formula, no new capability class is introduced.
This decision does not authorize a standalone "Department Gap score" —
there is no such single number. The workspace presents three existing
kinds of information, grouped by department, in one place.

## Decision 3 — Tenant Isolation and No Caller-Supplied Department Filter

Identical tenant-isolation pattern to every prior Phase 4 endpoint,
restated for this one:

```typescript
@Get('department-gap')
@UseGuards(JwtAuthGuard, RolesGuard)
@RequireRoles('System Administrator', 'HR Director', 'Workforce Planner')
async getDepartmentGap(@CurrentUser() actor: RequestUser) {
  return this.departmentGapService.getByTenant(actor.tenantId);
}
```

`tenantId` is never accepted from the request body, query string, path
parameters, or headers — always taken from `actor.tenantId`.

**The endpoint accepts no query parameters — in particular, no
`?departmentId=` filter.** This is a deliberate, governed design choice,
not an oversight: a caller-suppliable single-department filter would let a
role iterate department IDs one at a time, which is exactly the kind of
drill-down access pattern `GD-M32-1` Decision 3 already identified as a
re-identification risk. Instead, the endpoint always returns every
department in the tenant in one response (subject to the suppression rule
in Decision 6) — the same "return the full allowed set, let the frontend
select" pattern `VacancyRiskService` already uses for its ranked list, not
a new pattern.

## Decision 5 — Department-Level Formula Reuse (No New Formula)

`WorkforceReadinessService.scoreByDepartment(tenantId)` and
`AttritionRiskService.scoreByDepartment(tenantId)` compute the exact
formulas `GD-M31-1` Decision 5 and `GD-M32-1` Decision 5 already govern,
with the population filter changed from `tenantId` alone to
`tenantId AND departmentId` for every query the formula makes. No weight,
threshold, trailing-window length, or classification boundary changes.

```text
readiness-deterministic-v1, scoped per department:
  Staffing Coverage (0-30), Position Capacity (0-20), Vacancy Pressure
  (0-30, via VacancyRiskService reuse scoped to the department's positions),
  Certification Compliance (0-20) — identical weights, identical formula,
  GD-M31-1 Decision 5, department population instead of tenant population

attrition-deterministic-v1, scoped per department:
  Separation Rate (0-50), Tenure Composition (0-30), Position/Vacancy
  Recurrence (0-20) — identical weights, identical 365-day trailing
  window, identical formula, GD-M32-1 Decision 5, department population
  instead of tenant population

formulaVersion reported per department entry: the SAME identifiers already
in use ('readiness-deterministic-v1', 'attrition-deterministic-v1') — this
is not a new formula version, because it is not a new formula.
```

**Vacancy context (not a score):**

```text
Per department, DepartmentGapService computes, from data
VacancyRiskService's existing query already returns:
  openCount        = count of open/in-recruitment vacancies in the department
  criticalCount     = count of CRITICAL or HIGH riskLevel vacancies in the department
  avgDaysOpen       = average daysOpen across the department's scored vacancies
This is not a new score, not a new formula, and not subject to Decision 6's
suppression rule — see Decision 6 for why.
```

Any future change to any formula, weight, threshold, or window requires
its own governance decision amending `GD-M31-1` Decision 5 or `GD-M32-1`
Decision 5 directly — not a change smuggled into `scoreByDepartment()`
that diverges from `score()`. Both methods must always compute with
identical constants; only the population filter differs.

## Decision 6 — Minimum Headcount Suppression Rule

**This is the safeguard that makes department-level scoring safe where an
unscoped version was not.**

```text
Governed constant: MINIMUM_DEPARTMENT_HEADCOUNT = 5

A department's readiness and attrition scores are computed and returned
ONLY if the department has 5 or more employees meeting the same
"currently part of the workforce" population definition each underlying
formula already uses (GD-M31-1 Decision 5's active-workforce definition
for readiness; GD-M32-1 Decision 5's baselineWorkforce definition for
attrition — the two formulas may count slightly different populations per
their own existing governed definitions; each is checked against the
threshold independently for its own signal).

If a department's relevant population is below 5:
  readiness: null, attrition: null in that department's response entry
  suppressed: true
  suppressionReason: "Department population below minimum reporting
                       threshold (5)." — a fixed, non-identifying string;
                       never the actual headcount (see below)
  No factors, no confidence, no reasoning for the suppressed signal(s)

The actual headcount of a suppressed department must NOT be included in
the response, in the audit metadata, or anywhere else the frontend can
read it. Returning "this department has 2 employees" is only marginally
less identifying than returning a score computed from 2 employees — the
suppression must hide the population size, not just the score.

Rationale for the threshold value: 5 is a conventional minimum-cell-size
threshold in government statistical disclosure control practice. It is a
governed constant, changeable only by a new governance decision — not a
tunable parameter, not exposed via any API or configuration, and never
overridable per-role (System Administrator receives the same suppression
as every other allowed role; there is no role for which raw small-
department data becomes visible).

Vacancy context (Decision 5) is NOT subject to this suppression rule. A
vacancy is a position-level operational fact, not a person, and every
individual vacancy row within it is already fully visible to the same
SA/HRD/WP audience today via the existing Vacancy Risk Signals panel and
the Vacancy Risk tab. Aggregating vacancy counts by department introduces
no exposure beyond what already exists. This asymmetry (readiness/attrition
suppressed, vacancy context not) must be preserved — it is not an
oversight if a department shows vacancy context but a suppressed score.
```

## Decision 7 — Amendment to GD-M32-1 Decision 11: Narrowing the Department-Level Attrition Prohibition

`GD-M32-1` Decision 11 states: *"No department-level, team-level, or any
other small-group breakdown that could allow a reader to infer
individual-level information by elimination (e.g., a 3-person department's
'attrition risk' would functionally identify which of the 3 people is
driving the number)"* — and its Decision 13 restates this as *"prohibited
outright... not merely deferred."*

This decision formally narrows that prohibition, without reopening
`GD-M32-1`'s other decisions:

```text
GD-M32-1 Decision 11's department-level prohibition was written against an
UNSCOPED department breakdown — any department, any size, no safeguard.
The 3-person-department example it gave is precisely the failure mode
Decision 6 of this document (Minimum Headcount Suppression Rule) exists to
prevent. A 3-person department under this decision's rule is suppressed —
it never receives a score, and its population size is never disclosed.

GD-M32-1 Decision 11 remains fully in force for:
  The tenant-wide GET /api/v1/intelligence/attrition-risk endpoint —
    unchanged, still returns exactly one aggregate object, still has no
    department parameter (GD-M32-1 Decision 3, unchanged)
  Every role GD-M32-1 already forbids from any attrition signal
    (Recruiter, Hiring Manager, Compliance Officer) — unchanged, still
    forbidden from department-level attrition data by Decision 4 of this
    document
  Executive User — GD-M32-1 Decision 11's aggregate-only guarantee for EU
    is unchanged; EU still does not receive department-level attrition
    data under this decision (Decision 4 above)

What this decision narrows: department-level attrition scoring is now
permitted, but ONLY (a) for System Administrator, HR Director, and
Workforce Planner, (b) through the single new department-gap endpoint
governed by Decisions 2-6, and (c) subject to the minimum-headcount
suppression rule. It remains prohibited everywhere else this decision does
not explicitly authorize it.
```

Workforce Planner only, through the department-gap endpoint, subject to
the minimum-headcount suppression rule (Decision 6). `GD-M31-1`'s
tenant-wide `GET /api/v1/intelligence/workforce-readiness` endpoint, its
formula (Decision 5), its thresholds (Decision 6), and its Executive User
aggregation guarantee (Decision 11) are all unchanged by this decision.

## Decision 9 — Explainability Response Contract

`DepartmentGapService.getByTenant(tenantId)` returns a `department-gap-
response.dto.ts`-shaped payload. Each non-suppressed department entry
reuses the existing `IntelligenceExplainabilityOutput`/`RiskFactor` shape
(`GD-M30-1` Decision 8) for its readiness and attrition signals — no new
explainability contract is introduced.

```json
{
  "success": true,
  "data": {
    "departments": [
      {
        "departmentId": "uuid",
        "departmentName": "string",
        "suppressed": false,
        "readiness": {
          "readinessScore": 72, "readinessLevel": "DEVELOPING",
          "confidence": 90, "reasoning": "string",
          "factors": [ { "name": "...", "contribution": 0, "detail": "..." } ]
        },
        "attrition": {
          "attritionScore": 30, "attritionRiskLevel": "LOW",
          "confidence": 85, "reasoning": "string",
          "factors": [ { "name": "...", "contribution": 0, "detail": "..." } ]
        },
        "vacancyContext": {
          "openCount": 2, "criticalCount": 0, "avgDaysOpen": 14
        }
      },
      {
        "departmentId": "uuid",
        "departmentName": "string",
        "suppressed": true,
        "suppressionReason": "Department population below minimum reporting threshold (5).",
        "readiness": null,
        "attrition": null,
        "vacancyContext": { "openCount": 1, "criticalCount": 1, "avgDaysOpen": 20 }
      }
    ],
    "minimumHeadcountThreshold": 5,
    "computedAt": "ISO-8601 timestamp",
    "formulaVersion": "department-gap-deterministic-v1"
  }
}
```

`formulaVersion: 'department-gap-deterministic-v1'` identifies this
decision's grouping-and-suppression methodology specifically. It is
distinct from, and does not replace, the `readiness-deterministic-v1` and
`attrition-deterministic-v1` identifiers each non-suppressed department
entry still reports against the underlying, unmodified formulas (Decision
5). Departments are returned in a stable, non-comparative order
(alphabetical by department name, or by `Department.code`) — never sorted
by score, which would functionally rank departments (see Decision 13's
no-leaderboard rule).

## Decision 10 — AI Audit Event Policy

Every call to `GET /api/v1/intelligence/department-gap` must generate an
audit event, extending the pattern `GD-M30-1` Decision 9,
`GD-M31-1` Decision 9, and `GD-M32-1` Decision 9 established.

**New AuditEventType value to add:**

```typescript
// In apps/api/src/audit/enums/audit-event-type.enum.ts
// Added to the existing Intelligence Events block:

INTELLIGENCE_DEPARTMENT_GAP_QUERIED = 'INTELLIGENCE_DEPARTMENT_GAP_QUERIED',
```

**Audit event data to record:**

```typescript
await this.auditService.logEvent({
  tenantId:   actor.tenantId,
  userId:     actor.userId,
  entityType: 'intelligence',
  entityId:   undefined,              // aggregate; no single entity
  action:     AuditEventType.INTELLIGENCE_DEPARTMENT_GAP_QUERIED,
  result:     'SUCCESS',
  metadata: {
    formulaVersion:          'department-gap-deterministic-v1',
    departmentCount:         result.departments.length,
    suppressedDepartmentCount: result.departments.filter(d => d.suppressed).length,
  },
});
```

**Audit metadata must never include, under any circumstance:**

```text
Department IDs or names (individually — only the aggregate counts above)
Any individual department's score, level, or confidence
Employee IDs, names, or any candidate data
The actual headcount of any suppressed department
Any data that would let a reader reconstruct which specific departments
  were suppressed vs. scored
```

Only `formulaVersion`, `departmentCount`, and `suppressedDepartmentCount`
are permitted — an explicit key-set assertion in tests (matching the
`GD-M31-1`/`GD-M32-1` audit-metadata test pattern).

## Decision 11 — Aggregate-Only Guarantee and Individual-Level Prohibition (Restated at Department Scope)

This decision generalizes the individual-level prohibition `GD-M31-1`
Decision 11 and `GD-M32-1` Decision 11 established, to the department
scope this document introduces.

**Explicitly prohibited, for every allowed role, in M33 or as a side
effect of M33:**

```text
No per-employee readiness or attrition score, at any scope
No employee ranking within a department (e.g., "employees most likely to
  leave in this department")
No employee list or identifier of any kind (UUID, employee number, name,
  email) anywhere in the department-gap response or its audit metadata
No team-level or sub-department breakdown smaller than the Department
  entity itself (no shift, no manager-reporting-line grouping, no ad hoc
  grouping of any kind)
No manager-facing individual employee prediction of any kind
No disclosure of a suppressed department's actual headcount (Decision 6)
No department-vs-department ranking or comparison (Decision 13)
```

**What every allowed role (SA, HR Director, Workforce Planner) receives:**

Identical response shape and identical data for the same role set — there
is no role-conditional field stripping among the three allowed roles.
Executive User, Recruiter, Hiring Manager, and Compliance Officer receive
no response at all (403, Decision 4).

## Decision 13 — Department Gap Tab: Content and Layout (Frontend, Workspace-Only)

Extends `GD-M32-1` Decision 16, which authorized the Department Gap tab as
a disabled placeholder only. This decision enables real content:

```text
Layout (governed, not merely suggested):
  (a) A master-detail split, matching the existing Vacancy Risk tab
      pattern (GD-M32-1 Decision 18) exactly: a compact department list
      in a narrower column (department name, a compact suppressed/scored
      indicator, no score preview beyond what a single glance requires),
      and a single detail panel in a wider column showing the full
      readiness factor breakdown, attrition factor breakdown, and vacancy
      context for exactly one selected department at a time.
  (b) The department list is ordered alphabetically by department name (or
      department code) — never by score. Selecting a department updates
      only the detail panel; the list itself never expands, reorders on
      selection, or duplicates.
  (c) A suppressed department's list entry shows a muted, non-interactive-
      feeling (but still selectable, to show the suppression detail)
      indicator — selecting it shows the suppressionReason text and the
      vacancy context only; no readiness/attrition section renders for it.

Governed prohibition (restated from GD-M32-1 Decision 18 and extended):
  NO REPEATED STACKED-CARD DETAIL LAYOUT. NO DEPARTMENT LEADERBOARD OR
  RANKING VIEW. The tab must not render every department as its own
  sequential expanded card, and must not sort, rank, or visually emphasize
  departments by score. At most one department's full detail is shown at
  a time, via the master-detail selection pattern in (a).
```

## Decision 14 — Dashboard vs. Workspace Behavior (Governed Boundary)

```text
Main dashboard (apps/web/src/app/(dashboard)/dashboard/page.tsx):
  NO department-level data of any kind. This decision does not add, gate,
  or otherwise touch the dashboard. The existing Workforce Readiness,
  Attrition Risk, and Vacancy Risk Signals cards remain byte-for-byte
  unchanged in content, behavior, and layout. A department-level rollup on
  the dashboard would either require picking one department to feature
  (arbitrary and confusing) or a new visual pattern the dashboard's
  condensed-card design was never built to support — this decision defers
  that question entirely by keeping department data workspace-only.

Intelligence workspace (/intelligence, Department Gap tab):
  The only surface where department-level data of any kind is displayed.
  Factor contribution values and their governed maximums may be shown here,
  exactly as GD-M32-1 Decision 19 already permits for the Workforce
  Signals and Vacancy Risk tabs — this decision does not relax or extend
  that display rule beyond adding the Department Gap tab as a third place
  it applies. The same narrow proportion-indicator exception applies here
  and nowhere further; the underlying score itself is never a gauge or
  chart, on the dashboard or in the workspace.

No invented content, anywhere:
  Every value displayed in the Department Gap tab must trace directly to a
  field already present in the department-gap endpoint's response. This
  extends the existing "reasoning is never composed on the frontend"
  doctrine (GD-M32-1 Decision 19) to this tab.
```
