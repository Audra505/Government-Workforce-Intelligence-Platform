# Background

## Current Intelligence State

The platform has two intelligence signals: deterministic vacancy risk (M30),
visible to System Administrator, HR Director, and Workforce Planner; and
deterministic workforce readiness (M31), visible to those three roles plus
Executive User on an aggregate-only basis. No attrition signal of any kind
exists today. Recruiter, Hiring Manager, and Compliance Officer have no Phase 4
intelligence content in either M30 or M31 and are not granted any in M32.

## Attrition Risk Data Availability

All inputs required for a deterministic, **aggregate, tenant-wide** attrition
risk score already exist without any schema change:

```text
workforce.employees   — hireDate, terminationDate, employmentStatus
                         (canonical SEPARATED value per GD-M12-1), deletedAt
workforce.vacancies    — positionId, filledAt, createdAt, deletedAt
```

What does **not** exist, and is explicitly out of scope for M32 as a result:
there is no time-series/snapshot table recording headcount or separation
counts at regular historical intervals — only current-state rows with
`hireDate` / `terminationDate` / `employmentStatus` as of now. This is
sufficient for a **point-in-time aggregate ratio** (e.g., "what share of the
tenant's workforce separated in the trailing 365 days," "what share of the
current active workforce has short tenure") but is not sufficient for a true
multi-period trend analysis (FR-412-style "attrition trending up/down by
quarter"). This decision scopes the formula to what trailing-window aggregate
ratios can honestly support today; trend analysis remains deferred, consistent
with the Demand Forecasting Foundation option this milestone's planning report
also declined for M32 on the same underlying data-readiness gap.

## Why Deterministic, and Why Aggregate-Only

FR-402 does not mandate AI or LLM — it mandates that "prediction generated"
and "human review required" be satisfied. A deterministic formula satisfies
FR-900 (explainability) and FR-904 (audit) exactly as M30 and M31 already
proved, without external API dependencies, per-call cost, or non-reproducible
output.

Aggregate-only is a stronger constraint than M30 or M31 needed to state as
sharply, because attrition is a qualitatively more sensitive signal than a
vacancy's risk or a tenant's overall readiness percentage. A score that
predicts a *specific named employee's* likelihood of leaving is a judgment
about a real government employee that carries real risk: labor-relations
exposure, morale harm if ever leaked or misused, and a meaningful risk of
proxying for protected characteristics even when no protected characteristic
is a direct model input (tenure and department composition can correlate with
age, family status, or other protected traits in ways that are difficult to
fully audit away). The M30/M31 precedent of building services that are
*structurally* incapable of returning row-level data — not merely filtered
after the fact — is the precedent this decision leans on hardest. M32 follows
that precedent to its logical conclusion: `AttritionRiskService` never queries
for or returns a per-employee record in the first place. There is no
individual-level data in the response for any role to redact, because none is
ever computed in individually-identifiable form.

## Decision 3 — Tenant Isolation on the Attrition Risk Endpoint

Identical pattern to GD-M30-1 Decision 3 and GD-M31-1 Decision 3, restated for
this endpoint:

```typescript
@Get('attrition-risk')
@UseGuards(JwtAuthGuard, RolesGuard)
@RequireRoles('System Administrator', 'HR Director', 'Workforce Planner', 'Executive User')
async getAttritionRisk(@CurrentUser() actor: RequestUser) {
  return this.attritionRiskService.score(actor.tenantId);
}
```

`tenantId` is never accepted from the request body, query string, path
parameters, or headers — it is always taken from `actor.tenantId`, resolved
by `JwtAuthGuard` from the JWT. `AttritionRiskService` must filter every
Prisma query with `where: { tenantId, deletedAt: null }`, exactly as
`VacancyRiskService` and `WorkforceReadinessService` do. The endpoint accepts
**no query parameters** — no `?departmentId`, no `?windowDays`, no filter of
any kind. A department-scoped or otherwise filterable attrition endpoint
would materially increase individual-identification risk in a small agency
or small department (see Decision 11) and is not justified for M32. This is
not optional and cannot be relaxed — a cross-tenant leak in an aggregate
attrition score is still a SEC-003 violation.

## Decision 5 — Deterministic Attrition Risk Scoring Formula

`AttritionRiskService` computes one tenant-wide aggregate attrition risk score
per request, using the following governed formula. This formula version is
identified as `attrition-deterministic-v1`. Any future change to weights,
thresholds, inputs, or the trailing window length requires a new governance
decision and a new formula version identifier — the same discipline GD-M30-1
Decision 5 and GD-M31-1 Decision 5 established.

**Trailing window:** all time-bounded factors below use a governed trailing
window of **365 days** from the time of the request. This is a single fixed
constant for M32, not a caller-supplied parameter (Decision 3).

**Scoring inputs and factor weights (sum of all factors = attritionScore; max = 100):**

```text
Separation Rate Factor   (max 50 pts):
  trailingSeparations = count(Employee where tenantId matches, deletedAt
                         null, employmentStatus = 'SEPARATED', and
                         terminationDate within the trailing 365 days)
  baselineWorkforce = count(Employee where tenantId matches, deletedAt null,
                      and (employmentStatus != 'SEPARATED' OR
                      terminationDate within the trailing 365 days))
                      — i.e. everyone currently employed plus everyone who
                      separated within the window: the population that was
                      part of the tenant's workforce at some point during
                      the window
  separationRate = trailingSeparations / baselineWorkforce (0 if baseline is 0)
  separationFactor = round(min(1, separationRate / 0.30) * 50)
    — a trailing annual separation rate of 30% or higher reaches the factor
      cap; this ceiling is a governed constant, not derived from any
      external benchmark, and may only be changed by a new governance
      decision
  If baselineWorkforce is 0 (no one was ever part of this tenant's
  workforce in the window): separationFactor = 25 (neutral midpoint — see
  Decision 7, this also reduces confidence)

Tenure Composition Factor   (max 30 pts):
  activeEmployees = count(Employee where tenantId matches, deletedAt null,
                    employmentStatus in ('ACTIVE', 'ON_LEAVE',
                    'PENDING_ONBOARDING', 'SUSPENDED'))
                    — the current, non-separated workforce
  shortTenureCount = count of activeEmployees whose hireDate is within the
                     trailing 365 days (tenure < 1 year)
  shortTenureRatio = shortTenureCount / activeEmployees (0 if activeEmployees is 0)
  tenureFactor = round(shortTenureRatio * 30)
    — a higher share of short-tenure employees contributes more risk
      points; this is a structural composition signal, not a prediction
      about any individual employee's intent to leave
  If activeEmployees is 0: tenureFactor = 15 (neutral midpoint — see
  Decision 7, this also reduces confidence)

Position/Vacancy Recurrence Factor   (max 20 pts):
  eligiblePositions = distinct positionId values from Vacancy where
                      tenantId matches, deletedAt null, filledAt is not
                      null, and filledAt within the trailing 365 days
                      — positions that were filled at least once in the
                      window
  recurringPositions = count of eligiblePositions where the number of
                       distinct filled Vacancy rows for that positionId
                       within the trailing window is 2 or more
                       — the same position being vacated and refilled more
                       than once in a year, a churn proxy independent of
                       any individual employee
  recurrenceRatio = recurringPositions / count(eligiblePositions)
                     (0 if count(eligiblePositions) is 0)
  recurrenceFactor = round(recurrenceRatio * 20)
  If count(eligiblePositions) is 0 (no positions were filled in the
  trailing window): recurrenceFactor = 10 (neutral midpoint — see Decision
  7, this also reduces confidence)
```

**A fourth factor was considered and declined for M32.** The only readily
available additional structured signal — certification/skill non-renewal —
would substantially duplicate the Certification Compliance factor GD-M31-1
Decision 5 already established for workforce readiness, and no other
governed, non-duplicative, currently-modeled data source was identified.
Three factors summing cleanly to a 100-point maximum (50 + 30 + 20) is
sufficient for M32. Introducing a fourth factor in a future amendment
requires its own governance decision, including a full weight rebalancing
across all four factors, not an additive change to this formula.

**attritionScore:**

```text
attritionScore = min(100, separationFactor + tenureFactor + recurrenceFactor)
```

The maximum achievable score is 100 (50 + 30 + 20).

**factors array:**

The service must return a `factors` array itemizing each factor's
contribution, using the same `RiskFactor`-shaped entries GD-M30-1 Decision 8
defined (`name` / `contribution` / `detail`):

```json
[
  { "name": "separationRate", "contribution": 12, "detail": "7% trailing 12-month separation rate" },
  { "name": "tenureComposition", "contribution": 9, "detail": "30% of current workforce hired within the last 12 months" },
  { "name": "positionRecurrence", "contribution": 5, "detail": "1 of 4 filled positions refilled more than once in the last 12 months" }
]
```

Matching GD-M31-1 Decision 5's precedent (not GD-M30-1's), **all three
factors are always present** in this array — never omitted for a zero or
low contribution — because this is a whole-tenant aggregate score where the
reader must always see all of its inputs, not a per-row score where only
non-zero contributions are relevant.

## Decision 6 — Score Range and Attrition Risk Classification Thresholds

`attritionScore` is always an integer in the range 0–100 inclusive.

**Attrition risk classification thresholds:**

| attritionScore range | attritionRiskLevel |
|---|---|
| 0–24 | `LOW` |
| 25–49 | `MEDIUM` |
| 50–74 | `HIGH` |
| 75–100 | `CRITICAL` |

**This decision deliberately reuses the exact LOW/MEDIUM/HIGH/CRITICAL label
set and thresholds GD-M30-1 Decision 6 established for vacancy risk** — a
different choice from GD-M31-1 Decision 6, which deliberately invented a
distinct CRITICAL/AT_RISK/DEVELOPING/READY scale for workforce readiness.
The reason for the difference: readiness and vacancy risk run in *opposite*
directions (a high readiness score is good; a high risk score is bad), so
GD-M31-1 needed different labels to prevent misreading. Attrition risk and
vacancy risk run in the *same* direction — both are risk scales where a
higher score is worse — so reusing the identical scale is consistent and
lowers cognitive load for a user who already reads vacancy risk levels on
the same dashboard, rather than inventing a third label vocabulary for no
directional reason. These thresholds are governed values and must not be
adjusted in implementation without a new governance decision.

**No pagination, no filtering, no department breakdown:** the endpoint
returns exactly one score per request — the current tenant-wide attrition
risk snapshot. This is stricter than GD-M31-1 Decision 6 (which at least
left FR-411 department breakdown as a *deferred* future possibility) —
department-level attrition breakdown is explicitly *prohibited*, not merely
deferred, by Decision 11 of this document, because a small department's
breakdown can reveal individual-level information by arithmetic elimination.

## Decision 7 — Confidence Behavior and Data Insufficiency Handling

`confidence` is an integer 0–100 representing how reliable the computed
`attritionScore` is given available input data, following the same
philosophy as GD-M30-1 Decision 7 and GD-M31-1 Decision 7.

**Confidence rules:**

```text
Base confidence = 100.

Apply the following reductions, each independently, compounding
multiplicatively (not additively) when more than one applies:

  baselineWorkforce == 0 (Separation Rate Factor defaulted to neutral midpoint)
    → confidence × 0.5

  activeEmployees == 0 (Tenure Composition Factor defaulted to neutral midpoint)
    → confidence × 0.6

  count(eligiblePositions) == 0 (Position/Vacancy Recurrence Factor defaulted
  to neutral midpoint)
    → confidence × 0.7

  activeEmployees < 3 (small-sample statistical unreliability — distinct
  from the activeEmployees == 0 case above; both may apply)
    → confidence × 0.6

Final confidence = max(10, floor(compounded result))
  — confidence is never reported as 0; a floor of 10 signals "score exists
    but is very low confidence" rather than implying no score was computed
    at all, exactly as GD-M31-1 Decision 7 established.
```

**Data insufficiency rules (defensive, runtime):**

```text
Tenant has zero employees ever (no current workforce, no separation
history) and zero filled vacancies in the trailing window:
  attritionScore is still computed (all three factors default to their
  neutral midpoints per Decision 5) — the service must never throw.
  confidence will be at its compounded floor in this case.
  This is not an error condition — return the score with low confidence,
  not a 4xx/5xx response.

Any unexpected data shape (e.g., a terminationDate in the future, an
employmentStatus outside the canonical GD-M12-1 enumeration):
  Log a warning; exclude that single record from its ratio's numerator
  and denominator; never crash the response — matching GD-M30-1 Decision 7
  and GD-M31-1 Decision 7's "never throw an unhandled exception" doctrine.
```

```typescript
  confidence: number;
  reasoning: string;
  factors: RiskFactor[];
  computedAt: string;
  formulaVersion: string;   // 'attrition-deterministic-v1' for M32
}
```

**Naming note (implementation guidance, not a new interface):** exactly as
GD-M31-1 Decision 8 mapped the shared interface's generic `riskScore` /
`riskLevel` field names onto `readinessScore` / `readinessLevel` at the
response boundary, the `AttritionRiskResponseDto` returned to callers must
expose these as `attritionScore` and `attritionRiskLevel` in its own field
names. The shared interface itself is not renamed, to avoid touching M30's
contract.

**Full response shape for GET /api/v1/intelligence/attrition-risk:**

```json
{
  "success": true,
  "data": {
    "attritionScore": 26,
    "attritionRiskLevel": "MEDIUM",
    "confidence": 85,
    "reasoning": "Attrition risk is MEDIUM, driven primarily by tenure composition below target, offset by a low position recurrence rate.",
    "factors": [
      { "name": "separationRate", "contribution": 12, "detail": "7% trailing 12-month separation rate" },
      { "name": "tenureComposition", "contribution": 9, "detail": "30% of current workforce hired within the last 12 months" },
      { "name": "positionRecurrence", "contribution": 5, "detail": "1 of 4 filled positions refilled more than once in the last 12 months" }
    ],
    "computedAt": "2026-07-17T14:23:00.000Z",
    "formulaVersion": "attrition-deterministic-v1"
  }
}
```

The `reasoning` field must be dynamically composed from the actual factor
values at request time — it must never be a hardcoded template string
regardless of input, matching GD-M30-1's and GD-M31-1's reasoning-display
doctrine.

**This response shape is identical for every allowed role, including
Executive User** — see Decision 11.

## Decision 9 — AI Audit Event Policy

Every call to `GET /api/v1/intelligence/attrition-risk` must generate an
audit event, extending the pattern GD-M30-1 Decision 9 established and
GD-M31-1 Decision 9 continued.

**New AuditEventType value to add:**

```typescript
// In apps/api/src/audit/enums/audit-event-type.enum.ts
// Added to the existing Intelligence Events block:

INTELLIGENCE_ATTRITION_RISK_QUERIED = 'INTELLIGENCE_ATTRITION_RISK_QUERIED',
```

**Audit event data to record:**

```typescript
await this.auditService.logEvent({
  tenantId:   actor.tenantId,
  userId:     actor.userId,
  entityType: 'intelligence',
  entityId:   undefined,              // aggregate; no single entity
  action:     AuditEventType.INTELLIGENCE_ATTRITION_RISK_QUERIED,
  result:     'SUCCESS',
  metadata: {
    formulaVersion:     'attrition-deterministic-v1',
    attritionRiskLevel: result.attritionRiskLevel,

  },
});
```

**Audit metadata must never include, under any circumstance:**

```text
Employee IDs (of any kind — UUID, employee number, or otherwise)
Employee names
Candidate data of any kind (this endpoint has no relationship to candidates)
Department-level or any other small-group breakdown
Individual attrition scores (none are ever computed — see Decision 11)
Raw row counts that could allow the reader to reverse-engineer
  department- or team-level headcount changes not otherwise disclosed
```

Only the aggregate `attritionRiskLevel`, `confidence`, and `formulaVersion`
are permitted, exactly matching the metadata shape GD-M31-1 Decision 9
established for workforce readiness. This is structurally easy to guarantee
here for the same reason it was easy in M31: `AttritionRiskService` never
queries or returns row-level records to begin with, so there is no
individual data available to leak into the audit payload by mistake.

## Decision 11 — Aggregate-Only Guarantee and Individual-Level Attrition Prohibition (All Roles)

This decision satisfies GD-M30-1 Decision 11's requirement that any future
intelligence signal touching individual employees declare its data-sensitivity
posture in governance before implementation, and generalizes the aggregation
guarantee GD-M31-1 Decision 11 established for Executive User to **every**
role this endpoint serves.

**The aggregate-only guarantee (all roles):**

`GET /api/v1/intelligence/attrition-risk` returns exactly one tenant-wide
aggregate object per request, for every allowed role, with no
role-conditional field stripping and no per-employee data of any kind. This
guarantee holds structurally, not just by convention: `AttritionRiskService`
never queries for or returns row-level `Employee` records in individually-
identifiable form — every value it produces (`separationRate`,
`shortTenureRatio`, `recurrenceRatio`, and the resulting `attritionScore`) is
already a ratio or count computed across the whole tenant. There is no
individual-level data in the response for *any* role to redact, because none
is ever fetched into memory in individually-identifiable form in the first
place.

**Explicitly prohibited, for every role, in M32 or as a side effect of M32:**

```text
No per-employee attrition score
No employee ranking (e.g., "employees most likely to leave")
No employee risk list of any kind
No employee-specific explanation or reasoning tied to a named individual
No employee identifiers of any kind (UUID, employee number, name, email)
  anywhere in the attrition-risk response or its audit metadata
No department-level, team-level, or any other small-group breakdown that
  could allow a reader to infer individual-level information by
  elimination (e.g., a 3-person department's "attrition risk" would
  functionally identify which of the 3 people is driving the number)
No manager-facing or department-scoped employee attrition prediction of
  any kind — no role in M32, including Hiring Manager (who is forbidden
  from this endpoint entirely), receives any attrition signal scoped
  below the whole tenant
```

**Executive User receives:**

```text
The identical response shape and identical data as System Administrator,
HR Director, and Workforce Planner — attritionScore, attritionRiskLevel,
confidence, reasoning, factors, computedAt, formulaVersion.

No individual employee record or list.
No individual candidate record or list.
No individual vacancy row (the Vacancy Risk Signals panel itself remains
  forbidden to Executive User in M32, unchanged from M31).
No department-level or agency-level attrition breakdown (prohibited
  outright by this decision, not merely deferred — see Decision 6).
No new admin, workforce, or recruiting write/action capability of any kind.
No new navigation link, route, or shell access beyond the one dashboard card.
```

This is the second Phase 4 intelligence signal Executive User has ever
received (after M31's Workforce Readiness). Because the guarantee is
structural rather than a filter applied after the fact, there is no risk
class of "the filter was forgotten for one field" — the same simplest-
possible aggregation guarantee GD-M31-1 Decision 11 established, now
generalized as the explicit baseline for every future Phase 4 signal that
touches employee-level source data.

```text
  Any HTTP request to api.openai.com or any AI provider endpoint
```

versioning, cost tracking, failure handling, and confirmation that core
platform operations remain functional if the AI provider is unavailable —
none of that applies to M32, which introduces no such dependency.

```text
is simply unused by the dashboard's rendering of the same endpoint.
```

## Decision 19 — Factor Contribution Display Rule (Dashboard vs. Workspace)

This decision clarifies, and does not relax, GD-M30-1 Decision 10's original
prohibition on raw score-contribution math.

```text
Dashboard (unchanged, still fully prohibited):
  The condensed Workforce Readiness/Attrition Risk cards and the Vacancy
  Risk Signals table must continue to show NO raw factor-contribution math
  of any kind — no "+10", no "13/30", no point values — exactly as governed
  since GD-M30-1 Decision 10 and restated in GD-M31-1/GD-M32-1. This
  amendment does not touch that prohibition.

Intelligence workspace (newly permitted, this decision only):
  The workspace authorized in Decisions 15-18 is the "future dedicated
  intelligence page" GD-M30-1 Decision 10 explicitly reserved this detail
  for. Within the workspace ONLY, factor `contribution` values and their
  governed maximums may be displayed, as already returned by the existing
  M30/M31/M32 API responses.

Proportion indicators:
  A small proportion indicator (a thin fill bar showing contribution ÷
  governed maximum for a single factor row) is permitted within the
  workspace's factor tables only. This is not a chart, gauge, or sparkline
  in the sense the existing prohibitions intend: it decorates one
  already-numeric, already-governed data point per table row, carries no
  trend or forecast, and introduces no data beyond what the API already
  returns. It must never be applied to an aggregate score itself — the
  headline score remains a plain number plus a pill, never a gauge, on both
  the dashboard and the workspace.

No invented content, anywhere:
  The frontend must never invent, estimate, interpolate, or paraphrase a
  score, factor, formula name, or reasoning sentence, on the dashboard or
  in the workspace. Every value displayed must trace directly to a field
  already present in an existing M30/M31/M32 API response. This extends the
  existing "reasoning is never composed on the frontend" doctrine to the
  entire workspace.
```

## Decision 20 — RBAC: Intelligence Workspace Access Policy

```text
System Administrator, HR Director, Workforce Planner:
  Full access to the workspace, both tabs (Workforce Signals and Vacancy
  Risk). Matches their existing unrestricted access to all three M30/M31/M32
  signals.

Executive User:
  Access to the Workforce Signals tab ONLY (Workforce Readiness + Attrition
  Risk factor detail), matching their existing aggregate-only access to
  those two signals.
  FORBIDDEN from the Vacancy Risk tab. The master-detail layout exposes
  per-vacancy row-level detail (position title, department, individual
  factor breakdown) — row-level operational detail consistent with the
  existing, unchanged prohibition on Executive User ever seeing Vacancy
  Risk Signals content (GD-M30-1 Decision 4, unchanged by this amendment).
  If Executive User navigates to the workspace, the Vacancy Risk tab must
  not render for them (frontend), and GET /api/v1/intelligence/vacancy-risk
  continues to return 403 to Executive User (backend, unchanged).

Recruiter, Hiring Manager, Compliance Officer:
  No access to the Intelligence workspace in any form. The "Intelligence"
  top-navigation item itself must not render for these roles — matching
  the existing pattern where the dashboard's Workforce Intelligence section
  is silently omitted for them today.

Enforcement:
  Identical doctrine to every other Phase 4 surface — backend-enforced at
  the endpoint level (unchanged; no new endpoint is introduced by this
  amendment) with frontend gating for navigation/rendering as a UX
  convenience only, never as the sole protection.
```
